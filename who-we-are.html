The Practice

A studio of craft,
not contract.

LAPHIC Modern Homes is Uganda's design and construction studio — a small, principal-led team that draws, builds, and finishes under one roof. We work the way the best practices did a hundred years ago: listen first, draw twice, build once.

The result is homes, offices and gardens that age slowly — that quietly become better, year after year, because the joints were cut clean and the materials chosen for the long view.

How We Work

Four habits
that shape every project.

We're a small studio by choice. That gives us the room to keep four habits we won't compromise on — the ones that quietly make every project finish on time, on budget, and on the brief.

01

Listen First

Every project starts with a long conversation. About light, climate, family life, and the long view from the road — before a single line is drawn.

02

Draw Twice

We sketch, model, and refine until the design feels inevitable. Changes are cheap on paper, expensive on a slab.

03

Build Once

One contract, one accountable team — from foundation to handover. The same hands that designed it sign off every joint.

04

Stay In Touch

We return. A year later, five years later — we keep checking how a building wears, and we carry that learning into the next.
