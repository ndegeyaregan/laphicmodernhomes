The Practice

Landscape as architecture.

We treat every garden the way we treat a building — drawn carefully, built with patience, and planted for the way a place will look in ten years, not just on opening day.

From the boundary wall to the last flagstone, our landscape work brings the same craft to the open air as our interiors do to the rooms inside.

Featured · Gazebo
Outdoor Architecture

Built shade, built to last.

Gazebos, pergolas, summer kitchens and boundary pavilions — drawn into the landscape rather than placed on top of it. We work the structure into the levels, the planting, and the way light moves across the garden through the day.

  • Hardwood and steel structures detailed for the long view
  • Roofs designed for Uganda's sun and short, hard rains
  • Foundations that respect existing roots and drainage

Featured · Planting
Planting Design

A garden that belongs here.

We plant for Uganda — not for a magazine. That means native shade trees, hardy ornamentals, and grasses that come back after a dry month. The garden you walk into on handover day is only the first chapter; year three is the one we design for.

  • Indigenous trees and pollinator-friendly planting
  • Drought-tolerant beds, low-maintenance lawns
  • Soil prep, mulching and aftercare guidance
